Onboarding

Eight steps from recruit to active member. Complete them in order. Progress is saved in your browser.

4
Understand the SMART Rank System
// Know Your Path — Activity, Education, Contribution
▾

The Regiment uses the SMART promotion model. Rank is earned through three tracked categories: Activity (missions & training attendance), Education (completing courses), and Contribution (recruiting, peer evaluations, leading events). Type !rankreview in Discord when you're ready for a promotion review.

5
Community Member vs Squad Member
// Choose Your Path — Understand your commitment level
▾

Community Member: Flexible participation. Join voice channels, engage in discussions, invite others, join events at your own pace. No formal rank obligations.

Squad Member: Structured gameplay. Join a squad for dedicated training, missions, and leadership development. Earn ranks, specialize in roles, and progress toward leadership positions. Access private squad channels and participate in competitive PVP and GM Missions.

6
Lux Platoon & Squad Structure
// Know Your Unit — Squads, hierarchy, assignment
▾

The Regiment operates under Lux Platoon. Hierarchy runs: CO → Platoon Leader → Squad Leader → Team Leader → Squad Members. Active squads include LUX 1-1 (Reaper, Phoenix, Pale Horse), Last Resort (Oasis, Geronimo), Death or Liberty, and Regiment 1 Tactical Group.

7
Events, Schedule & Discord Navigation
// Stay Active — Know when and where things happen
▾

Battle Day — Every Friday (server codes in #server-codes-only). Game Master Missions — Saturdays, modded immersive ops. PVP Events — Scheduled competitive matches (requires 1–2 training sessions). Monthly Onboarding — 5th Sunday of each month. Essential channels: #regiment-announcements, #questions-and-answers, #regiment-forms, #ranks-and-roles, #active-squads.

8
Onboarding Presentation & Live Session
// Final Briefing — Review the deck, attend the session
▾

Review the official Regiment Onboarding Presentation on Canva — it covers mission, values, squads, events, and member expectations. Then attend the live monthly onboarding session (5th Sunday of each month) in the Game Master Briefings voice channel. Questions? Post in #questions-and-answers or DM @[CO]WSSIWARLORDI.

// Progression Doctrine

SMART Rank System

Rank is earned, not given. Three categories: Activity, Education, Contribution. Type !rankreview in Discord to request a promotion review via MEE6.

SL Eval Form ↗
The 3 Promotion Categories
SMART Model
🎯 Activity
Monthly
Attendance in missions and training sessions, tracked monthly. Consistency is the baseline for all promotions.
📚 Education
Courses
Completing specific courses — Combat Basics, Leadership I & II, Logistics, Recon Specialist, Game Master Module.
🤝 Contribution
Impact
Recruiting new members, peer evaluations, leading events, managing squad channels, mentoring junior members.
Junior Enlisted — E-1 through E-4
Foundation
E-1

Private

Entry rank on squad assignment. Attend 1 mission or training/month. Follow Rules & CoC. Learn movement, comms, and team roles.

→ To PFC: 3 weeks active · 2 squad trainings + 2 missions · 60%+ attendance · SL Eval Form + Feedback Form
E-2

Private First Class (PFC)

3 weeks as Private. 2 squad trainings, 2 official missions, SL Eval form, Regiment Feedback form. 60%+ attendance, active in regiment channels.

→ To LCPL: 3 weeks as PFC · 3 squad trainings · 2 missions/PVPs · Point Man or 2IC in 2 missions · Complete Combat Basics · Recruit 1 member
E-3

Lance Corporal (LCPL)

3 weeks as PFC. Act as Point Man or 2IC in 2 missions. Complete Combat Basics Course. Recruit 1 new member.

→ To CPL: 30 days as LCPL · FTL in 2 GM/Public missions · 2 Regiment courses · 2 PvP events · Co-host 1 training · Recruit 2 active members
E-4

Corporal (CPL)

30 days as LCPL. FTL in 2 missions. 2 Regiment Courses. 2 recruits (active 2+ weeks). Co-host 1 training. 2 PvP or Major Op events.

→ To SGT: 30 days as CPL · 3 squad trainings · FTL/Squad 2IC in 3+ missions · Leadership Basics Course · 5+ total recruits · Mentor 1 Private
NCO Corps — E-5 through E-9
Leadership
E-5

Sergeant (SGT)

30 days as CPL. Lead as FTL or Squad 2IC in 3+ missions. Leadership Basics Course. 5+ total recruits. Mentor 1 Private through onboarding.

→ To SSGT: 30 days · Lead 6+ missions as SL/TL · Leadership Course II + Logistics Specialist · 9 recruits · 2 NCO meetings in 60 days · Training Tracker
E-6

Staff Sergeant (SSGT)

30 days as SGT. Lead 6+ missions/PVPs as SL or TL. Manage fireteam of 3+ or host 2+ trainings/month for 3 months. 9 total recruits. 70% squad approval if SL.

→ To GYSGT: 45 days · Lead 8+ missions as SL/Platoon 2IC · Recon Specialist + GM Module · 12 recruits · Staff 1 Regiment-wide event · Regiment role for 60 days · Community Roles
E-7

Gunnery Sergeant (GYSGT)

45 days as SSGT. Lead 8+ missions OR become Drill Instructor OR full-time Recruiter. 2 structured platoon trainings. 12 total recruits. Manage a regiment responsibility role for 60 days.

Roles: Community · Patreon · Recruiter Form
E-8

Master Sergeant (MSGT)

60 days as GYSGT. Lead 5+ missions/PVP or 12+ public matches. Host major Regiment training. Hold 3 specialist certifications. GM Module (Proficient). Mentor 2 SSGT/GYSGT. 15+ recruits. Lead 1 Regiment project. 80% squad eval response if SL.

Training Form · Module Form · SL Eval
E-9

Sergeant Major (SGM)

60 days as MSGT. Lead 12+ missions, 4 PVPs, or 18+ public matches. All basic courses complete. Promote 2 TLs to SL. 25+ recruits. Manage 40+ member channel + 2 additional channels. 75% squad approval.

OR: Recruit 32+ members · OR host 20+ training sessions via Training Form
Leadership Paths
Two Options
Option 1 — Independent Squad
CPL+ Required
8–12 committed members · Minimum CPL or 45 days in Regiment · Own squad identity (name & logo) · Not active in another squad · Coordinate with current SL if applicable.
Leadership Application ↗
Option 2 — Fireteam Leader
LCPL+ Required
Already in a squad · Minimum LCPL rank · Recruit 3–5 members (all must complete Recruit Form) · Coordinate with your Squad Leader · Run operations consistently with your fireteam.

SOP Library
Expandable
Keep comms short, clear, and relevant. No cross-talk during contact.
  • Use callouts: direction, distance, target type, urgency.
  • Leader comms take priority over all others.
  • Confirm orders with short read-backs.
  • No music or excessive noise on mic during ops.
Orders flow down. Feedback flows up. No freelancing in ops.
  • CO → Platoon Leader → Squad Leader → Team Leader → Operator.
  • Squad Lead owns squad execution during ops.
  • Fireteam Lead owns movement and contact handling.
  • Operators execute, report, and support.
If you sign up, show up. If you can't make it, notify leadership early.
  • Be on time for briefing — late arrivals disrupt the op.
  • Follow mission ROE and objectives as briefed.
  • No drama during ops — resolve issues through proper channels.
  • Submit AAR feedback if requested by leadership.
Training is how we keep quality high and performance consistent.
  • Complete Combat Basics before first deployment — non-negotiable.
  • Role certifications are required for specialized role assignments.
  • Leaders must maintain attendance and readiness at all times.
  • Squad Leaders must host regular training sessions for their unit.
